Home Weed Dog Blog/My Thoughts

I have recently lost my grandfather and I have been thinking a lot about mortality and life.
Life is a fickle thing and to know how easily and quickly it can be taken is somewhat frightening, however
it is also slightly relieving in an odd way. To know that, on top of life being something that naturally comes and goes,
that it also something that largely doesn't matter in the grand scheme of everything. As good or as bad of a mindeset as
this may be, it gives me a sense of calmness, as it means that I don't have to worry as much about how I lead my life.
I am able to focus more on the enjoyment of life, instead of the typical stresses of "what if I don't do something great?"
"What if I don't live up to expectations?" because in the end none of it will matter. We will all just be disappated energy,
going off into the space around us.
This gives me a sense of calm, a sense that it will be alright in the end.
A releiving knowledge that even if I don't succeed, in a 100 years nobody will be around to remember it all.


Michael Puckett, April 4th, 2022.
